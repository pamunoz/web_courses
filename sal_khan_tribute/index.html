Salman Khan

Educator who founded the Khan Academy

Salman Amin "Sal" Khan (born October 11, 1976) is a Bangladeshi-American educator who founded the Khan Academy, a free online education platform and an organization with which he has produced over 6,500 video lessons teaching a wide spectrum of academic subjects, mainly focusing on mathematics and sciences.

Here's a time line of Salman Khan's life:


  • 1976 - Born in Metairie, Louisiana, U.S.
  • 1998 - Khan attended the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, graduating with Bachelor of Science degrees in mathematics, electrical engineering, and computer science, later getting his Master of Science in electrical engineering and computer science. Khan was class president in his senior year.
  • 2002 - Khan was a summer intern at PARC. ,
  • From 2003 to late 2009 - Khan worked as a hedge fund analyst at Connective Capital Management.
  • 2003 - Khan began tutoring his cousin, Nadia, in mathematics over the internet using Yahoo!'s Doodle notepad.
  • 2006 - When other relatives and friends sought his tutoring, he moved his tutorials to YouTube where he created an account.
  • 2009 - The popularity of his educational videos on the video-sharing website prompted Khan to quit his job as a financial analyst
  • 2011 - Salman Khan was invited to speak at TED by Bill Gates who says he uses Khan Academy Exercise Software to teach his own children.
  • 2012 - Khan Academy, initially a tool for students, added the Coach, promoting the connection of teachers with students through videos and monitor tools.
